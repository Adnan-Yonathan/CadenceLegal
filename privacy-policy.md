Cadence Privacy Policy
Effective date: June 29, 2026
Cadence: Run Coach ("Cadence", "we", "our", or "us") helps runners create training plans, track runs, understand recovery, receive coaching, use route and trail tools, and manage Cadence Pro membership access.
This Privacy Policy explains what information Cadence uses, why it is used, and the choices you have.
Information Cadence Uses
Cadence may use the following information depending on the features you choose:
Profile and onboarding information, such as your name or handle, running goals, experience level, preferred units, preferred running surface, weekly training schedule, coach tone, coaching focus areas, commitment pact, and training preferences.
Training and progress information, such as training plans, scheduled workouts, streaks, ranks, achievements, XP, personal records, and coaching history.
Run and workout information, such as run date, duration, moving time, distance, pace, splits, route coordinates, elevation, cadence, estimated energy, heart rate when available, workout type, ghost-race results, target pace results, shoes, weather notes, mood, perceived effort, pain or soreness notes, and free-form notes you enter.
Recovery and readiness information, such as manual recovery score, sleep hours, sleep quality, energy level, body feel, soreness, pain timing, injury check-ins, and related recovery inputs.
Apple Health information, if you choose to connect Apple Health, such as sleep, heart rate, resting heart rate, heart rate variability, VO2 max, body mass, walking/running distance, active energy, and recent workouts. Cadence may also write completed Cadence workouts, distance, and active energy to Apple Health if you grant write permission.
Location information, if you grant location access, such as live GPS coordinates, route coordinates, pace and distance data, trail approach distance, and active-run location updates. Background location is used only during an active run when you grant Always location permission.
Photos you choose, such as a selected image for share cards or images saved to your photo library.
Purchase and subscription information, such as Cadence Pro entitlement status, subscription restore status, and paywall interaction status used to unlock access.
Device and app information needed to operate the app, such as app settings, selected theme, units, permissions status, and technical information generated by Apple system services and our subscription/paywall provider.
How Cadence Uses Information
Cadence uses information to:
Provide onboarding and membership access.
Generate and adapt training plans.
Calculate readiness, recovery, and training load.
Track runs, routes, splits, pace, distance, Live Activities, and run history.
Provide adaptive coaching, post-run insights, and coach responses.
Support ghost runs and target-pace comparisons.
Build and save routes and trails.
Create share cards when you choose to use sharing features.
Process, restore, and unlock Cadence Pro subscriptions.
Improve app reliability and support user-requested troubleshooting.
Cadence is not a medical device and does not provide medical advice. Recovery and training guidance is for fitness and informational purposes only.
Apple Health
Apple Health access is optional. Cadence asks for Health permissions only when you choose to connect Apple Health. You can grant or revoke individual Health permissions in the Health app or iOS Settings.
Cadence uses Health data only to provide fitness, recovery, readiness, training-load, and run-tracking features. Cadence does not use Health data for advertising.
Location and Background Location
Cadence uses location to map runs, calculate distance and pace, build routes, support trail approach, and update Live Activities during active runs.
If you grant Always location permission, Cadence uses background location only while a run is actively in progress so distance, pace, route, and Live Activity information can continue when the phone is locked or Cadence is in the background.
Photos and Sharing
Cadence accesses photos only when you choose a photo for a share card or choose to save a generated share card to your photo library. Photos you select may be stored locally in the app as part of your profile or share-card data.
Purchases and Subscription Providers
Cadence Pro subscriptions are processed through Apple in-app purchase. Cadence uses purchase and restore status to show paywalls, manage access, and unlock Cadence Pro.
Cadence also uses Superwall to present subscription screens and manage paywall/entitlement status. Superwall and Apple may process information under their own terms and privacy policies.
Storage and Sharing
Cadence stores app data on your device using local app storage unless a system service or provider is used for a feature you choose, such as Apple Health, Apple Photos, Apple in-app purchase, Live Activities, or Superwall.
Cadence does not sell your personal information.
Your Choices
You can:
Change Health permissions in the Health app or iOS Settings.
Change Location permissions in iOS Settings.
Change Photos permissions in iOS Settings.
Manage Live Activity and notification permissions in iOS Settings.
Manage or cancel subscriptions in your Apple App Store account settings.
Delete Cadence from your device to remove locally stored app data from that device, subject to iOS and Apple account behavior.
Children
Cadence is not intended for children under 13.
Changes
We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will update the effective date or provide notice where appropriate.
